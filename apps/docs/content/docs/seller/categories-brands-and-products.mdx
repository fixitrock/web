## Main Catalog Pages

| Route | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `/@username/products` | Add, search, and manage products |
| `/@username/categories` | Create and organize product categories |
| `/@username/brands` | Create and manage brands |
| `/@username/stocks` | Track available stock if enabled for your role |

## Recommended Order

1. Create or clean up brands first.
2. Add categories that match how customers search.
3. Add products with clear naming, pricing, and media.
4. Verify the public products tab from `/@username?tab=products`.

## Product Writing Rules

- Lead with brand and model information.
- Keep naming consistent across the catalog.
- Use images that actually match the product being sold.
- Avoid mixing firmware-like naming with storefront product naming unless the item is truly a file-based product.

## Categories

Good categories make both the public storefront and the seller search experience easier.

Examples:

- Display
- Battery
- Charging IC
- FRP Files
- Driver Packs

## Brands

Brands help with filtering, public clarity, and future search improvements.

If your catalog mixes physical spare parts and digital repair files, keep the brand names clean and avoid duplicate spellings.

## Publish Check

After any major catalog edit:

1. Refresh the public storefront.
2. Open the products tab as a customer would.
3. Confirm category filtering works.
4. Make sure pricing and images are still aligned.
